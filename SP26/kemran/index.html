How Tomorrow Might Look

Color and Composition - Integrating Design Principles

For this project, I revisited one of my earliest designs and reimagined it through a 3×3 grid, which helped me discover new relationships between shapes and see the design from a fresh perspective. By experimenting with different color palettes and applying Gestalt principles such as symmetry, continuity, and similarity, I learned how structure and color work together to create rhythm, unity, and emotion. The final composition combined color theory, visual hierarchy, and refined forms, turning a simple redesign into an exploration of perception and creative growth.

Utilizing Mockups

Clear is Kind - Designing for Usability

For my online learning portal prototype, I used rounded icons and updated the navigation bar with rounded corners after usability testing feedback, creating a more consistent and modern interface. I improved accessibility by choosing high-contrast colors and simplified the home page layout after paper prototype testing showed that users found it confusing. To make the experience more interactive, I added animated icons, a search option for finding assignments, smoother page transitions, and error pages that help users recover without losing progress.
